Analytical Framework
====================

Why this approach?
------------------

When facing complex business problems, there is a natural tendency to jump directly into KPIs, operational issues or proposed solutions.

I deliberately chose a different approach.

Before attempting to improve a system, I first wanted to understand:

*   What is the system?
    
*   What does an effective version of that system look like?
    
*   What organizational capabilities sustain it?
    
*   What makes those capabilities fail?
    

Only after answering these questions did I move towards diagnosis and recommendations.

Principles behind the framework
-------------------------------

1.  Systems should be understood before they are optimized.
    
2.  Organizational capabilities matter more than isolated KPIs.
    
3.  Operational friction accumulates at transitions rather than individual stages.
    
4.  Improvements should strengthen the structure instead of treating isolated symptoms.
    
5.  Automation should never precede standardization.

Part I — Building the Analytical Framework

**Objective**

Create a common mental model before discussing problems.

**Slides**

Purpose

Define the Lead-to-Cash process as an interconnected system.

Main ideas

*   Think in systems.
    
*   Transitions matter more than isolated stages.
    
*   Optimization starts with understanding.

Purpose

Main ideas

Part II — Diagnosing the Current State
--------------------------------------

Slide 4

Purpose

Translate qualitative feedback into structural weaknesses.

Ideas

*   Feedback becomes evidence.
    
*   Symptoms point towards root causes.
    
*   Organizational capabilities are measurable.
    

Slide 5

Purpose

Locate operational bottlenecks.

Ideas

*   Performance deteriorates at transitions.
    
*   KPIs reveal where friction accumulates.
    
*   Every bottleneck is mapped to its underlying capability.
    

Part III — Decision Making
--------------------------

Slide 6

Purpose

Select the initiative that strengthens the largest number of foundational capabilities simultaneously.

Ideas

*   Prioritize structural impact.
